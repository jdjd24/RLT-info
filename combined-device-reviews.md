# Red Light Method: Device Reviews

## How these reviews work

**Panels** are evaluated as body-coverage tools. The practical question is how much of the body can be treated per session, at a consistent distance, without turning the routine into a multi-step setup. Size and coverage therefore carry the most weight, followed by value relative to size and usable output.

**Masks** are evaluated as face-coverage tools. Here, comfort, fit, and coverage usually matter more than headline power, because adherence over months is the limiting factor in real use.

**About power and EMF**  
Irradiance is only meaningful when distance and measurement context are stated. Manufacturer-only claims are treated conservatively. EMF is scored on how specific the claim is, not on vague reassurance language.

# Full-body panels and full-body configurations

## 1) Mito 72" Column (2x MitoPRO 1500+)

**Overall: 8.3 / 10**  
Scores: Power 8 | Value 7 | EMF claims 7 | Features 8 | Modularity 9 | Size/Coverage 10

**Specs**

- Wavelengths: 630 / 660 / 830 / 850 nm
- LEDs / size / weight: 600 LEDs; 72" x 12" x 3"; ~44 lb
- Irradiance: >170 mW/cm2 at 6" (manufacturer-stated)
- Controls: red-only, NIR-only, combined modes; timer
- Price (USD): ~$2,238
- Warranty / trial: 3-year warranty; 60-day trial
- Safety positioning: marketed as Class II / OTC wellness device

**Quick verdict**  
This is one of the few consumer configurations that is plausibly full body in a single stance. The score is driven by coverage and reduced setup friction rather than higher headline output.

**Design and practical implications**  
A 72" vertical column changes session geometry. Most of the body remains inside the treatment field at once, which reduces repositioning and distance drift. That matters more in practice than small differences in quoted irradiance.

**Strengths**

- True head-to-toe coverage for most adults.
- Fewer session steps, fewer geometry variables.
- Built from a modular system that can be serviced or reconfigured.

**Limitations**

- Large, heavy, and space-demanding.
- Irradiance figures are manufacturer-presented; no public third-party map of the assembled column.

**Scoring rationale**  
Size/Coverage is the defining advantage (10/10). Value is good relative to coverage but still a large absolute spend (7/10). Power is solid but limited by lack of independent mapping (8/10). Modularity is high because this is a mature ecosystem build (9/10).

**What would change the score**  
A published multi-point irradiance map of the full column face at stated distances would materially strengthen confidence.

# Facial masks and face devices

(Prices normalized to USD where possible.)

**Overall: ~6.8 / 10**

**Specs**

- Wavelengths: 630 nm (red), 880 nm (NIR), 605 nm (amber), 415 nm (blue)
- LED count: ~162 LEDs (100 red mode + 62 blue mode)
- Session time: 3 minutes, auto shutoff
- Modes: red, blue, combination
- Price (USD): ~$455

- Clear wavelength disclosure.

**Limitations**

- No irradiance-at-skin data.
- Rigid shell limits fit adaptability.

# Bottom line

Across panels, coverage per session and value per coverage dominate real-world usefulness. That is why the Mito columns and the MitoPRO 1500+ score highest overall. Across masks, comfort, coverage, and routine feasibility dominate, which is why flexible silicone masks cluster near the top despite weak power transparency.
