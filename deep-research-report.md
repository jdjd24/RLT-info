## How we compared devices and how we score them

### Measurement sanity check

Irradiance is the key “power at the tissue” metric most consumers look for, but panel irradiance numbers are frequently **not comparable** because brands change:
- **Distance** (6 inches vs 12 inches vs unstated), which can change readings dramatically. citeturn46view4turn42search9  
- **Meter type and bandwidth** (consumer “solar” meters vs photodiode sensors vs spectroradiometric methods), which can under-read or mis-read certain wavelengths. citeturn45view0turn43search1  
- **Whether irradiance is a “sum across wavelengths”** or a per-wavelength measurement, sometimes hidden behind fine print. citeturn43search1turn42search10  
- **Beam uniformity and sampling method** (a single hot spot can mislead; averaging across multiple points is more honest). citeturn43search3  

For that reason, this review never treats “LED wattage” as performance, and treats manufacturer-only irradiance claims conservatively unless distance and method are clear. citeturn46view4turn45view0turn42search10

### Power confidence tiers

- **Tier A**: independent measurements with distance + method + multi-point sampling clearly documented.  
- **Tier B**: partial independent data (some methodology, but missing key elements such as distance, calibration, or full-face/panel mapping).  
- **Tier C**: manufacturer-only claims, or claims that are distance-unstated or method-unstated.

### Scoring weights

All category scores are **0 to 10**. The **Overall Score** is the weighted composite below (weights sum to 100%):

- **Size / Coverage**: 30%  
- **Price (value relative to size + usable power)**: 25%  
- **Power**: 15%  
- **Features**: 10%  
- **Modularity**: 10%  
- **EMF Claims**: 10%  

This intentionally weights what most determines real-world adherence: “how much you can treat per session per pound spent”, not “what the spec sheet says”. citeturn46view4turn45view0

How to read these cards:
