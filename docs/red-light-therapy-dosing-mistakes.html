Dosing

Red light therapy dosing mistakes to avoid

Most poor outcomes come from inconsistent routine design, wrong distance assumptions, or chasing intensity without considering total weekly dose.

Key takeaways

  • Mistake 1: Using device wattage instead of measured irradiance at your real treatment distance.
  • Mistake 2: Doing long sessions inconsistently instead of shorter repeatable sessions.
  • Mistake 3: Ignoring total weekly dose and drifting into underdosing or overdosing.

Fixes that work

ProblemPractical fix
No reliable dose baselineUse the calculator and measured irradiance.
Too much protocol complexityUse one repeatable protocol for 4-6 weeks before changing variables.
Poor adherenceChoose device type by realistic routine friction, not spec-sheet ambition.
